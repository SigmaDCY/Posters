### 1. 故事简介（Main Feature）

**HEADLINE: Ordinary Girl, Extraordinary Courage: Annemarie's Story**

In 1943, Copenhagen, Denmark, is a city under Nazi occupation. Soldiers are everywhere. For 10-year-old Annemarie Johansen, life has become full of fear and rules. But when her best friend, Ellen Rosen, who is Jewish, is threatened with being "relocated" by the Nazis, Annemarie's family risks everything to save her.

Suddenly, Annemarie is thrown into a world of secrets, hiding, and dangerous meetings with soldiers. She must be brave, even when she is terrified. The story follows her journey from a carefree girl to a key helper in the Danish Resistance. It is a powerful story about how true bravery means doing what is right, even when you are scared.

---

### 2. 人物聚焦（Character Spotlight）

**HEADLINE: Best Friends in a Broken World**

- **Annemarie Johansen**: The main character. She is thoughtful, brave, and quick-thinking. At the beginning, she is a normal girl, but the war forces her to grow up fast. Her love for Ellen is her greatest strength.
- **Ellen Rosen**: Annemarie's Jewish best friend. She is practical and proud of her heritage. To escape the Nazis, she has to pretend to be Annemarie's dead sister, Lise. Imagine having to forget your own name just to stay alive!

**Question for readers**: How does Annemarie change from the start of the book to the end? What events make her braver?

---

### 3. 历史知识窗（Historical Background）

**HEADLINE: The Real Story Behind the Fiction**

Did you know that Lois Lowry's book is based on true events? When the Nazis ordered all Danish Jews to be arrested, the people of Denmark fought back—not with guns, but with courage and community.

- **The Great Escape**: In just a few weeks, ordinary Danish citizens helped smuggle over 7,000 Jewish people—almost all of Denmark's Jewish population—across the sea to neutral Sweden.
- **A Secret Code**: The phrase "I will wear my pink ribbon today" was not just a game. It was a real code used by the Resistance to warn Jewish families that they had to hide immediately.
- **The King's Symbol**: There is a famous story (though probably not true) that King Christian X of Denmark wore a yellow Star of David to show support for the Jews. This story shows the spirit of unity that the book celebrates.

---

### 4. 读后感 / 书评（Review & Reaction）

**HEADLINE: Why You Should Read This Book**

*Number the Stars* is set more than 80 years ago, but its message is still important today. It is not a long or difficult book, but every chapter is full of tension. The scenes where Annemarie is stopped by soldiers while carrying a coffin will make you hold your breath.

This book makes you ask yourself: **What would I do?** Would I have the courage to lie to a soldier? To help a friend even if it could cost my life? It is a story about friendship, sacrifice, and the idea that even small acts of bravery can make you a hero.

**Rating: ★★★★★ (5 out of 5 Stars)**

---

### 5. 经典语录与解读（Famous Saying）

## 三、英文文章内容（可直接抄写）

### 1. 故事简介（Main Feature）

**HEADLINE: A Voice That Survived: The Story of Anne Frank**

Anne Frank was a Jewish girl living in Amsterdam during World War II. On her 13th birthday, she received a diary—a simple red-checked notebook that would become one of the most important books in history. She named it “Kitty.”

Weeks later, her life changed forever. When her sister Margot received a call-up notice from the Nazis to be sent to a labor camp, the Frank family went into hiding. They moved into a “Secret Annex” behind Otto Frank’s office building, together with the van Pels family and later a dentist named Fritz Pfeffer. For over two years, eight people lived in hidden rooms, never able to make a sound during the day, never able to go outside.

In her diary, Anne wrote about everything: her fears, her hopes, her arguments with her mother, her growing feelings for Peter van Pels, and her dreams of becoming a writer. She wrote: *“I want to go on living even after my death.”* Her wish came true—but not in the way she imagined.

In August 1944, the Annex was betrayed. Everyone was arrested and sent to concentration camps. Of the eight, only Otto Frank, Anne’s father, survived. After the war, he published Anne’s diary. Today, it has been read by millions of people around the world.

---

### 2. 人物聚焦（Character Spotlight）

**HEADLINE: The People in Hiding**

- **Anne Frank**: The author. She is energetic, talkative, curious, and opinionated. She dreams of becoming a journalist or a writer. She has a difficult relationship with her mother but is very close to her father.
- **Margot Frank**: Anne’s older sister. She is quiet, obedient, and well-behaved—the “perfect” daughter. Anne sometimes feels jealous of her.
- **Otto Frank (Pim)**: Anne’s father. He is calm, kind, and wise. He is the leader of the group in hiding and Anne’s greatest supporter.
- **Edith Frank**: Anne’s mother. She is more formal and critical of Anne. Their relationship is complicated, but she loves her daughters deeply.
- **The van Pels Family** (called van Daan in the diary): Mr. van Pels works with Otto. He is smart but sometimes selfish. Mrs. van Pels complains a lot. Their son, Peter, is shy and quiet—Anne later develops a crush on him.
- **Fritz Pfeffer** (called Albert Dussel in the diary): A dentist who joins later. He shares a room with Anne and drives her crazy with his strict habits.

**Question for readers**: If you had to live in hiding with seven other people for two years, who would be the hardest to get along with?

---

### 3. 历史背景 & 安妮的原话（Historical Context & Anne‘s Own Words）

**HEADLINE: Life in the Secret Annex — In Anne‘s Own Words**

Use real excerpts from the diary to show what life was like. Here are three short passages with simple explanations .

**Excerpt 1 — The First Day in Hiding (July 10, 1942)**

> “Our living room and all the other rooms were so full of stuff that I can’t find the words to describe it. All the cardboard boxes that had been sent to the office in the last few months were piled on the floors and beds. … Father and I, the two cleaner-uppers in the family, started in right away.” 

*What this shows*: The hiding place was not ready when they arrived. They had to work hard just to make it livable. Even in danger, life went on with ordinary tasks.

**Excerpt 2 — A Funny Moment (May 9, 1944)**

> “Dussel now sits on the ‘can,’ to borrow the expression, every day at twelve-thirty on the dot. This afternoon I boldly took a piece of pink paper and wrote: Mr. Dussel’s Toilet Timetable … I tacked this to the green bathroom door while he was still inside.” 

*What this shows*: Even in hiding, Anne kept her sense of humor. This story about bathroom schedules shows how eight people had to share tiny spaces—and how Anne used her wit to cope.

**Excerpt 3 — A Taste of Freedom (July 8, 1944)**

> “I kept thinking, ‘Are we really in hiding?’ This must be how it feels when you can finally go out into the world again.” 

*What this shows*: This is from a day when helpers came upstairs to make strawberry jam. For a moment, the Annex felt almost normal. But the doorbell rang—danger was always near.

---

### 4. 读后感 / 书评（Review & Reaction）

**HEADLINE: Why Anne‘s Story Still Matters**

Anne Frank was just a teenager. She argued with her mom, daydreamed about boys, and worried about her homework. But she also lived through something unimaginable: two years in hiding, total silence during the day, the constant fear of being caught.

What makes her diary so powerful is that she wrote about both. She wrote about the war, but she also wrote about growing up. She was scared, but she also wrote: *“In spite of everything, I still believe that people are really good at heart.”*

Reading her diary, you feel like you know her. You laugh at her jokes about Mr. Dussel. You feel her excitement when she gets her first kiss from Peter. And then you remember: she never left the Annex alive. She died in Bergen-Belsen at age 15, just weeks before the camp was liberated.

Her voice reaches across 80 years and says: *I was here. I was real. This happened.*

**Rating: ★★★★★ (5 out of 5 Stars)** — Not just a book, a piece of history.

---
